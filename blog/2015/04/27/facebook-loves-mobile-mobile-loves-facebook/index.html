Facebook loves mobile. Mobile loves Facebook
Pitchtarget Apr 27 2015

Day after day we hear of success stories from mobile advertisers that use Facebook to drive more installs and conversions with brilliant results. It's not all about the ads: Facebook Platform comes to help in order to drive more users and better user trust.

Mobile App Install

Mobile App Install Ads appear in the Mobile News Feed just like normal Page Posts do. You have a similar real estate, but practically every tap leads to the App Store or Google Play. Even the Call To Action, "Install Now", leaves no interpretation. It is a great way for users to discover new app – and to install them without leaving Facebook.

Here's why we think it's a great solution:

  • Having more clickable surface means maximising clicks while optimising for installs.
  • Combine Mobile App Install Ads with oCPM for the best results. Some advertisers may even be able to specify their target CPI.
  • You can use images or even videos to promote your app, which is particularly useful when you are promoting new apps.

Targeting and Mobile Custom Audiences

Apart from the obvious targeting options (such as the ability to specify device platform and operating system), there are some interesting behaviors that you can use in order to maximise efficiency:

  • Device connection. You can deliver ads only to people who are browsing from a Wi-Fi connection, or to people browsing from their 3G/LTE connections. Use this option if your app size may clash with your users' data caps, and you should be able to improve your Click-to-Convert rate.
  • Technology early adopters. This behavior is a set of people who logs into Facebook from brand new devices.
  • Mobile Custom Audiences. By using Mobile Custom Audience, you will be able to further segment you app users and to retarget them based on actions they made (or did not make). For instance, you can target people who played your first three levels of a game but did not use the app afterwards.

Facebook Login and Open Graph

Remember, there is still free distribution on Facebook and both your apps and your users can benefit from it.

Facebook Login allows people to signup to your app by using their Facebook identity. This means that users will not need to type and verify emails, and they will not need to create or remember a password. And then you reduce friction during the signup process, you will naturally see a decrease in your churn rate.

You should also encourage people to share content from you app back to Facebook. This does not require Facebook Login: the Facebook SDK gives you plenty of options and most of them are straightforward to implement into your app. When your users share content from you app, their friends will see those posts. People on mobile will be prompted to install the app, which means that even more installs may come to your app!
